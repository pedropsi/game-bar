<h2>About the «TITLE-BOLD»</h2>
<p>All «NAME»'s <a href="games.html">puzzlescript games</a> make use of a "game bar" with features such as a undo/redo buttons, level selector, a music on/off button and a fullscreen button. </p>

<h2>How to use the «TITLE-BOLD» in your site</h2>
<h3>Step 1</h3>
<p>Simply include your game page the <b>javascript source</b> and the <b>stylesheet</b>:</p>
<ul>
	<li>The <b>javascript source</b>: <a href="public/GameBar/GameBar.js">GameBar.js</a></li>
	<li>The <b>stylesheet</b>: <a href="public/GameBar/GameBar.css">GameBar.css</a></li>
</ul>
<p>The <b>javascript source</b> must be loaded <b>after</b> the game loads completely, because a few of the original game functions are overwritten (mostly because of the level selector).</p>

<h3>Step 2</h3>
<p>Ensure the  <b>javascript source</b> can find your game, by editing the line (near the end):
<code>var containerID="#game";</code>.
The selector named <code>"#game"</code> should be replaced by a different selector matching the container (div) holding your game (canvas). Often this container matches the class selector <code>".gameContainer"</code>.</p>

<h3>Step 3</h3>
<p>Customise the <b>stylesheet</b> to agree with your page's style and game selectors.</p>
<p> Don't forget to replace selectors <code> .game-container {etc...};</code>, <code> .game {etc...};</code> and <code> #gameCanvas {etc...};</code> with the appropriate selectors for your page.</p>

<p>Currently, only in games where among all levels, only a single level contains all checkpoints (e.g. a open world map). If multiple levels have checkpoints, it may not work correctly.</p>
